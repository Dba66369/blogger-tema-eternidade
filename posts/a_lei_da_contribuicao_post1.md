# Lei da Contribuicao: Prosperidade Atraves do Dar Generoso

## Introduction: The Spiritual Principle of Giving

Among all the biblical principles that govern prosperity and spiritual growth, few are as profound and consistently affirmed as the Law of Contribution. From the teachings of the Old Testament to the words of Jesus Christ in the New Testament, the principle that generous giving leads to greater blessing is woven throughout scripture. This is not merely a religious concept but a spiritual law that operates in the lives of believers who embrace it with faith and obedience. In this comprehensive guide, we will explore the biblical foundation of contribution, its practical applications, and how embracing a lifestyle of generous giving can transform your spiritual and financial life.

## Biblical Foundations of the Law of Contribution

The Law of Contribution finds its roots in the very beginning of biblical teaching on stewardship. In the book of Genesis, the patriarch Abraham gave a tenth of everything to Melchizedek, the priest of the Most High God, establishing the principle of tithing long before it became codified in the Mosaic Law. This act of giving was not commanded but flowed from Abraham grateful heart and recognition that all he had came from God. The tithe, or tenth, became the standard of contribution in ancient Israel, but the spirit behind it was always about acknowledging God as the source of all provision.

In the New Testament, Jesus elevated the concept of giving beyond mere percentages and rules. His teaching in Matthew 23:23 emphasizes that while tithing is important, it should not overshadow the weightier matters of justice, mercy, and faithfulness. Jesus observed the wealthy casting large sums into the temple treasury and then noticed a poor widow who gave two small copper coins. He declared that she had given more than all the others because she gave everything she had. This teaching establishes that the value of contribution is measured not by the amount given but by the sacrifice and heart of the giver.

The Apostle Paul, in his letters to the Corinthians, provides perhaps the most detailed New Testament teaching on giving. In 2 Corinthians 9:6-7, he writes that whoever sows sparingly will also reap sparingly, and whoever sows generously will also reap generously. Each person should give what they have decided in their heart to give, not reluctantly or under compulsion, for God loves a cheerful giver. This passage establishes several key principles: giving should be generous, voluntary, joyful, and proportionate to what God has provided.

## The Spiritual Mechanics of Contribution

Understanding how the Law of Contribution works requires recognizing it as a spiritual principle rather than a simple transaction. When believers give, they are participating in a divine exchange where faith is expressed through action. The act of giving demonstrates trust that God will continue to provide, even as resources leave one possession. This is the essence of faith in action: releasing what is held with confidence that God honor the act of generosity.

The principle of sowing and reaping is central to understanding contribution. Just as a farmer must sow seed to harvest a crop, believers must give to receive. The Bible consistently teaches that generosity activates God blessing in a person life. This is not a prosperity gospel guarantee of material wealth but a spiritual reality that God blesses those who honor Him with their resources. The blessing may come in various forms: financial provision, wisdom, relationships, health, or spiritual growth.

Another spiritual dimension of contribution is its power to break the spirit of greed and materialism. When believers practice regular giving, they train their hearts to value eternal treasures over temporary possessions. The discipline of giving creates a spiritual posture of abundance rather than scarcity, even when circumstances suggest otherwise. This shift in mindset from lack to abundance is one of the most transformative aspects of the Law of Contribution.

## Practical Applications: How to Give Generously

### Tithing as a Foundation

Tithing, the practice of giving ten percent of one income, serves as the foundational level of contribution for most believers. While the New Testament does not command tithing with the same legal force as the Old Testament, it remains a wise spiritual discipline. Setting aside the first ten percent of income acknowledges God as the primary source of provision and establishes a baseline of regular, proportional giving. Many believers find that tithing becomes the gateway to greater generosity and blessing in their lives.

### Offering Beyond the Tithe

Beyond the tithe, believers are encouraged to give offerings as their hearts are moved and as they are blessed. Offerings are given for specific purposes: supporting missions, helping those in need, building church facilities, or advancing kingdom work. These additional contributions flow from a heart of gratitude and a desire to participate in God work. Unlike tithing, which is a regular commitment, offerings respond to specific needs and opportunities.

### Giving to Those in Need

One of the most direct expressions of generous giving is providing for those in need. The Bible repeatedly commands believers to care for the poor, the widow, the orphan, and the stranger. Jesus identified with the needy so completely that He said whatever is done for the least of His brothers is done for Him. Regular giving to charitable causes, supporting ministries that serve the vulnerable, and helping individuals in crisis are all practical expressions of the Law of Contribution.

### Supporting Kingdom Work

Believers are called to support the work of the church and its ministries. This includes financial support for pastors and church staff, funding for outreach programs, contributions to missions, and support for Christian education and discipleship. Every dollar given to kingdom work is an investment in eternal outcomes, multiplying far beyond what can be seen in this life.

## The Blessings of Generous Giving

The Bible promises multiple dimensions of blessing for those who give generously. Financial blessing is often the most discussed, with passages like Malachi 3:10 promising that God will open the windows of heaven for those who bring the full tithe. Proverbs 19:17 declares that whoever is kind to the poor lends to the Lord, and He will reward them for what they have done. Luke 6:38 teaches that with the measure used to give, it will be measured back, pressed down, shaken together, and running over.

Beyond financial blessing, generous giving produces spiritual growth and maturity. The discipline of giving cultivates gratitude, trust, and dependence on God. It breaks the power of greed and creates a heart posture that values eternal treasures. Many believers report experiencing greater peace, joy, and spiritual clarity as they embrace generous giving.

Generosity also opens doors for greater ministry and influence. Those who give faithfully often find themselves entrusted with greater resources and opportunities to serve. God entrusts more to those who have proven faithful with what they have been given. This principle of faithfulness leading to greater stewardship is a consistent theme in both the Old and New Testaments.

## Conclusion: Embrace the Law of Contribution Today

The Law of Contribution is not a burdensome obligation but an invitation to participate in God generous nature. Every act of giving is an opportunity to express faith, demonstrate gratitude, and position oneself for greater blessing. Whether through tithing, offerings, helping those in need, or supporting kingdom work, generous giving transforms both the giver and the receiver. The path to prosperity begins with a willing heart and open hands. Start today, give cheerfully, and watch as God honors your faithfulness with blessings beyond what you can imagine.
